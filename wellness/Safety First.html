Safety First

Comprehensive safety guidelines for responsible pet ownership. Always prioritize professional veterinary care.

Common Pet Toxins & Poisons

Human Foods

  • Chocolate: Contains theobromine, toxic to dogs and cats
  • Grapes & Raisins: Can cause kidney failure in dogs
  • Onions & Garlic: Damages red blood cells
  • Xylitol: Artificial sweetener in gum, candy, baked goods
  • Alcohol: Highly toxic, even in small amounts
  • Caffeine: Coffee, tea, energy drinks
  • Macadamia Nuts: Toxic to dogs
  • Avocado: Persin toxin affects birds, rodents

Household Products

  • Cleaning products: Bleach, disinfectants, detergents
  • Antifreeze: Ethylene glycol - sweet taste attracts pets
  • Rodenticides: Rat and mouse poisons
  • Insecticides: Bug sprays, ant baits, flea treatments
  • Medications: Human pain relievers, antidepressants
  • Essential oils: Tea tree, eucalyptus, pennyroyal
  • Fertilizers: Garden and lawn products
  • Batteries: Acid and heavy metal exposure

Toxic Plants

  • Lilies: Extremely toxic to cats - all parts
  • Sago Palm: All parts, especially seeds
  • Azalea/Rhododendron: Grayanotoxin poisoning
  • Oleander: Cardiac glycosides affect heart
  • Castor Bean: Ricin toxin - extremely dangerous
  • Autumn Crocus: Colchicine causes organ damage
  • Dieffenbachia: Calcium oxalate crystals
  • Tulips & Hyacinths: Bulbs most toxic

Emergency Response for Suspected Poisoning

1. Remove your pet from the source immediately
2. Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by a professional
3. Call your veterinarian or pet poison helpline immediately
4. Bring the container/plant with you to the vet
5. Note the time of exposure and amount ingested

Pet First Aid Guide

1

Assess the Situation

Check for danger to yourself and your pet first

Approach carefully - injured animals may bite

Check responsiveness - gently call name, touch

Look for obvious injuries - bleeding, limping

2

Check Vital Signs

Breathing: Normal rate (dogs: 10-30/min, cats: 20-30/min)

Heart rate: Place hand on chest behind left elbow

Gum color: Should be pink (not white, blue, or yellow)

Capillary refill: Press gum, color should return in 1-2 seconds

3

Basic First Aid Procedures

CPR for pets: Only if no heartbeat AND no breathing

Bleeding control: Apply direct pressure with clean cloth

Burns: Cool with running water, don't apply ice

Choking: Look in mouth, perform Heimlich if trained

Heatstroke: Move to cool area, wet with cool water

4

First Aid Kit Essentials

Gauze pads & bandages
Adhesive tape
Blunt-end scissors
Digital thermometer
Syringe (no needle)
Antiseptic wipes
Hydrogen peroxide (3%)
Emergency contact list

Travel Safety Checklist

Car Travel

• Use pet seatbelt or carrier
• Never leave pet in hot car
• Take breaks every 2-3 hours
• Bring water and travel bowl

Air Travel

• Check airline policies in advance
• Get health certificate from vet
• Use airline-approved carrier
• Book direct flights when possible

Hotel Stays

• Confirm pet-friendly policies
• Bring familiar bedding
• Never leave pet unattended
• Clean up after your pet

Outdoor Adventures

• Keep on leash in new areas
• Check for ticks frequently
• Bring pet-safe insect repellent
• Watch for wildlife hazards

International Travel Requirements

Documentation

• Current rabies vaccination
• Health certificate (APHIS Form 7001)
• Import permits if required
• Microchip documentation

Quarantine

• Research destination requirements
• Some countries require quarantine
• Plan well in advance
• Consider pet's stress levels

Seasonal Safety Guide

Summer Safety

  • Heatstroke prevention: Never leave in hot cars
  • Paw protection: Hot pavement can burn pads
  • Water safety: Not all dogs can swim naturally
  • Parasite control: Fleas, ticks, mosquitoes
  • Lawn chemicals: Wait before allowing access
  • BBQ hazards: Skewers, bones, corn cobs

Winter Safety

  • Antifreeze: Sweet taste attracts, highly toxic
  • Ice melt: Can irritate paws, toxic if ingested
  • Hypothermia: Limit outdoor time in extreme cold
  • Indoor heating: Space heater safety
  • Car engines: Cats seek warmth
  • Dry skin: Humidifier may help

Spring & Fall

  • Allergies: Pollen, mold, seasonal
  • Mushrooms: Many wild varieties are toxic
  • Rodenticides: Increased use in cooler months
  • Holiday hazards: Halloween candy, decorations
  • Snakes: More active during mild weather
  • Seasonal plants: Bulbs, decorative plants

Holiday Safety

  • Christmas: Tinsel, ornaments, lights
  • Thanksgiving: Turkey bones, rich foods
  • Easter: Chocolate, lilies, plastic grass
  • Fireworks: Noise phobia, escape risk
  • Candles: Open flame hazards
  • Guests: Food, medications, escape doors
